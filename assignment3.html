Assignment 3 -
3D printing & scanning

3D printing

Project outline

The objective of this assignment is to 3D pint anything we come up with. The only requirements is that it can not use more than 100 grams of material and make something which would be hard to make using subtracive manufacturing methods.

Before we get started

Prior to starting the assignment a decision had to made regarding what type of 3D printer should be used. The facility where the 3D printing takes place has three types of printer as listed on the homepage of the course. These three are as follows:

  • Creality CR-10 MAX
  • Prusa MK3+
  • Ultimaker 3+

    • As far as I can tell all of these printers are built in to the Fusion 360 library so that is not a deciding factor. I decided to go for the Creality CR-10 MAX. When researching which printer would be most ideal, there was an article i stumbled upon which stated that the Creality printer is the best suited for beginners (which I am at this stage)

    Testing

    Before you can start designing and printing your object, it is wise to start with a test object. I found a test object on thingiverse, which includes:

  • Support test
  • Scale test
  • Overhang test

  • Bridging test

    Design

    I thougt about it for a while what I wanted to make for my 3D printing project. However, everything I could come up with was either not within the boundaries of the assignment or just plain boring/ugly. Finally I decided what I wanted to make.

    One of my friends is a major bottled drinks enthusiast, so I decided to make him a bottle opener with a twist, the twist being that it's a picture of his face.

    First I needed to find a good image of him. I decided to use this one:

    Next I imported the image into inkscape, turned it into a vector graphic (to see how that is done, check out assignment 2 . I then exported it as a .dxf file so it could be opened in Fusion 360. Then all I had to do was to touch up the lines and add the bottle opener. This is the final design:
